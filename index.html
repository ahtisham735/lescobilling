Simplifying Your Electricity Management: A Guide to LESCO Online Services

Check Your LESCO Bill

1. How to Check LESCO Bill Online

Gone are the days of waiting in long queues or dealing with paper bills. With LESCO's online bill checking service, you can access your electricity bill anytime, anywhere. Simply follow these steps:

  1. Visit the LESCO website.
  2. Locate the 'Bill Inquiry' or 'Online Billing' section.
  3. Enter your 14-digit reference number (found on your bill).
  4. Click on 'Submit' or 'View Bill' to access your current bill details.
  5. You can also choose to download or print the bill for your records.

2. How to Pay LESCO Bill Online

Paying your electricity bill is quick and convenient with LESCO's online payment options. Here's how you can settle your bill electronically:

  1. Visit LESCO's official website.
  2. Navigate to the 'Online Bill Payment' section.
  3. Enter your reference number or consumer ID.
  4. Select your preferred payment method (credit/debit card, mobile wallet, or online banking).
  5. Follow the prompts to complete the payment securely.
  6. Upon successful transaction, you'll receive a confirmation message.

3. How to Apply for a New LESCO Connection

If you require a new electricity connection, LESCO offers an online application process for your convenience. Follow these steps:

  1. Visit the LESCO website and find the New Connection section .
  2. Fill out the online application form accurately.
  3. Attach the necessary documents, such as CNIC, proof of ownership, and site plan.
  4. Submit the application and await LESCO's verification process.
  5. Once approved, proceed to pay the required fees online.
  6. LESCO will schedule the installation of your new connection.

6. Tips to Reduce LESCO Bill

If you aim to decrease your electricity bill, consider implementing these energy-saving practices:

  • Utilize energy-efficient appliances and lighting.
  • Turn off lights and electronics when not in use.
  • Opt for natural ventilation instead of air conditioning.
  • Seal gaps and cracks to prevent energy loss.
  • Explore the possibility of installing solar panels or other renewable energy sources.

7. Safety Guidelines

When dealing with electricity, prioritizing safety is paramount. Adhere to these essential safety guidelines:

  • Avoid tampering with electrical equipment or meters.
  • Engage qualified professionals for electrical installations and repairs.
  • Keep electrical outlets and wires away from water sources.
  • Utilize surge protectors to safeguard sensitive electronics.
  • In the event of emergencies or power outages, promptly contact LESCO's helpline.
